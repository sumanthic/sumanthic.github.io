# sumanthic

A personal dev journal — written for me, public by accident.

## Purpose

Capture learnings, experiments, and things I figured out so I don't forget them.
Not tutorials. Not thought leadership. Just honest notes from the work.

## Audience

Primarily: future me.
Secondarily: anyone who finds it via search and is stuck on the same thing.

## Voice

- First person, casual
- No pressure to be comprehensive — a rough note published beats a perfect post never written
- Links to sources; credit where due

## What goes here

- Things I spent time figuring out that weren't obvious
- Experiments: what I tried, what worked, what didn't
- Summaries of papers, talks, or books I want to internalize
- Tools, configs, or workflows I want to remember

## What doesn't go here

- Marketing content
- Polished tutorials written for SEO
- Opinions on industry drama

## Homepage

The homepage **is** the archive — a chronological list of posts grouped by year, no excerpts, no hero section.
Not a feed. Not a landing page. Just: most recent posts first.

## Done looks like

A live URL where I can write a markdown file, push, and see it published as a readable post within minutes.
No features needed beyond that until they're actually missed.

## Identity

- **Blog name:** sumanthic
- **Nav logo:** `< sumanthic />`
- **Live URL:** https://sumanthic.github.io
- **GitHub:** Points to sumanthic's GitHub profile (About nav link resolves here until an About page exists)

## Inspiration

[vanducng.dev](https://vanducng.dev/posts/) — minimal, content-first, chronological archive.
Reference for aesthetic, not feature parity. We build a strict subset.
